# Alchemist — Turn a Plain Service Into an Irresistible Offer

## What This Skill Does

Alchemist is Agent 2 of the Momentum System. It reads the validated market from Hunter and transmutes a plain service description into a Grand Slam Offer (GSO) using Alex Hormozi's complete framework from $100M Offers. It produces a named, priced, guaranteed offer with a full value stack — something that cannot be compared to a competitor's price. Saves `offer.md` to the project folder.

A commodity offer competes on price. A Grand Slam Offer forces "your offer vs. nothing" — the prospect either buys or doesn't. Price comparison becomes irrelevant.

---

## Hormozi Knowledge — Offer Frameworks

### What Is a Grand Slam Offer ($100M Offers)

A Grand Slam Offer is an offer so good the prospect would feel stupid saying no. It cannot be compared to any other product or service in the market — it forces the "your offer vs. nothing" decision rather than a price comparison. Hormozi attributes all of his 8–9 figure business success to creating 4–5 Grand Slam Offers over his career.

A commodity offer: "Social media management — $1,500/month." A GSO: "The 90-Day Booking Machine — guaranteed 10 new booked calls per month or I work for free until you do."

### The 5-Step Grand Slam Offer Creation Process

**Step 1 — Dream Outcome:** Identify the destination, not the journey. What does the customer's life look like AFTER the work is done? Frame it as a status transformation. Not "I'll set up your email automation" but "You wake up to qualified leads in your inbox without touching a keyboard."

**Step 2 — Obstacle List:** List every reason a buyer might hesitate, sorted into 4 doubt categories:
- Financial doubt: "Is it worth the money?"
- Likelihood doubt: "Will it actually work for me specifically?"
- Effort doubt: "Is this going to be hard on my end?"
- Time doubt: "How long before I see results?"

**Step 3 — Solutions:** Reverse each obstacle into a solution, feature, or component. Each solution directly neutralizes the specific doubt it addresses.

**Step 4 — Delivery Vehicles:** Assign each solution a delivery method:
- DFY 1:1 — done for them, just for them (highest perceived value)
- DWY 1:1 — done with them, just for them
- DIY 1:1 — they do it, you guide 1:1
- DFY 1:many — done for them, for a group (low marginal cost)
- DIY 1:many — they do it, group support (near-zero marginal cost)
Apply the 10x/1/10th test: Does this make the result 10x more likely? Does it take 1/10th the effort from the client?

**Step 5 — Trim and Stack:** Remove high-cost/low-value items. Keep low-cost/high-value items. Assign a standalone dollar value to each component. Stack the total — it must be at minimum 5x the actual price.

### The Value Equation

**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort and Sacrifice)**

Maximize the numerator:
- Dream Outcome: specific, vivid, status-relevant
- Perceived Likelihood: what increases confidence (testimonials, guarantees, track record, specificity)

Minimize the denominator:
- Time Delay: fast early wins + explicit timeline
- Effort and Sacrifice: how much the client has to do (DFY zeroes this out)

Elite marketers compete on the denominator. "Fast beats free." Perception drives the equation — framing the benefit as status gained from others' viewpoints multiplies Dream Outcome.

### MAGIC Naming Formula

**M** — Magnetic reason why (what makes this timely?)
**A** — Avatar callout (who is this for, named directly?)
**G** — Goal (what specific outcome?)
**I** — Interval (in how long?)
**C** — Container word (System, Sprint, Blueprint, Accelerator, Session, Machine)

Use 3–5 components. Bonus for rhyme or alliteration. Examples: "90-Minute AI OS Quick-Start," "The Solo Coach Booking Machine," "7-Day Lead Sprint."

### Guarantee Types

- **Conditional guarantee** (recommended for services): "If you do X, Y, Z and don't get [result], I'll [remedy]." The conditions protect the provider; the remedy is meaningful to the buyer.
- **Unconditional guarantee** (B2C info products): "Love it or refund." High-trust, zero friction — risky for service businesses.
- **Anti-guarantee** (info products): "No refunds — if you're the type who asks for refunds, this isn't for you." Attracts serious buyers.
- **Implied guarantee** (agencies, B2B): "I keep working until you get [result]." No formal refund, but commitment to the outcome.

### Premium Pricing Principle

Charge more → clients are more invested → they get better results → your reputation improves → you charge more. The inverse (vicious cycle): lower prices → price-sensitive clients → worse results → forced to lower prices further.

Hormozi example: Gym Launch charged $16k–$42k/year when competitors charged $500–$5k/month. Result: $239k/year average topline increase per gym. The price change improved client results, not just margins.

## Step 2: Dream Outcome (Step 1 of GSO)

Identify the destination, not the journey. Frame it as a status transformation — what does the customer's life look like AFTER the work is done?

Use the Value Equation numerator: maximize Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood.

Ask: What is the gap between where they are now and where they want to be? Frame the outcome in their language (from the avatar's exact words in `market.md`). This is what you sell — not the steps to get there.

Example: not "I'll set up your email automation" but "You wake up to new leads in your inbox without touching a keyboard."

---

## Step 3: Obstacle List (Step 2 of GSO)

List every reason a buyer might hesitate. Sort into the 4 doubt categories:

**Financial doubt** — "Is it worth the money?"
**Likelihood doubt** — "Will this actually work for me specifically?"
**Effort doubt** — "Is this going to be hard / time-consuming on my end?"
**Time doubt** — "How long before I see results?"

Generate at least 2 obstacles per category. Be honest — these are the real objections that kill deals.

---

## Step 4: Solutions (Step 3 of GSO)

Reverse each obstacle into a solution, feature, or component of the offer.

Each solution should directly neutralize the specific doubt it addresses. Examples:
- Financial doubt "Is it worth it?" → Solution: ROI calculator showing 10x return at minimum engagement
- Likelihood doubt "Will it work for me?" → Solution: "Done-With-You setup call — I don't leave until it's live"
- Effort doubt "Too hard?" → Solution: "I do the entire technical setup; you answer 3 questions"
- Time doubt "How long?" → Solution: "First result visible in session 1, within 90 minutes"

---

## Step 5: Delivery Vehicles (Step 4 of GSO)

Assign each solution a delivery method using the DFY/DWY/DIY × 1:1/1:many matrix:

- **DFY 1:1** — done for them, just for them (highest perceived value, highest cost to deliver)
- **DWY 1:1** — done with them, just for them (high perceived value, medium cost)
- **DIY 1:1** — they do it, you guide 1:1 (medium perceived value, low cost)
- **DFY 1:many** — done for them, for a group (medium perceived value, low marginal cost)
- **DIY 1:many** — they do it, group support (lower perceived value, near-zero marginal cost)

Apply the **10x/1/10th test**: Does this delivery vehicle make the result 10x more likely? Does it take 1/10th the effort from the client? Prefer delivery methods that score high on both.

---

## Step 6: Trim and Stack (Step 5 of GSO)

Review every component:

- Remove: high-cost-to-deliver AND low-perceived-value items
- Keep: low-cost-to-deliver AND high-perceived-value items

Assign a standalone dollar value to each kept component (what would someone pay for this alone?). Be honest but generous — you are pricing the value to the client, not your time.

Stack them into a total. The total should be at minimum 5x the actual price.

---

## Step 7: Value Equation Audit

Run the offer through Hormozi's Value Equation:

**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

Audit each variable:
- **Dream Outcome**: Is it specific, vivid, and status-relevant? Weak = vague benefit. Strong = named transformation.
- **Perceived Likelihood**: What increases confidence? (testimonials, guarantees, track record, credentials, specificity of the mechanism)
- **Time Delay**: Is there a fast early win? Is the timeline explicit?
- **Effort & Sacrifice**: How much does the client have to do? DFY zeroes this out.

Flag any variable that is weak. Prescribe a fix.

---

## Step 8: Guarantee

Draft a conditional guarantee tailored to the business type.

**Conditional guarantee format:** "If you [do X, Y, Z] and don't [get specific result], I will [specific remedy]."

The conditions should be achievable by a motivated client. The remedy should be meaningful but not ruinous. Examples:
- "If you complete all 3 setup sessions and don't have at least one AI workflow running by the end of session 3, the last session is free."
- "If we don't identify at least 5 automation opportunities in your audit, you pay nothing."

Tune the guarantee to remove the biggest doubt type identified in Step 3.

---

## Step 9: MAGIC Naming

Apply the MAGIC formula to generate 3 offer name options:

- **M** — Magnetic reason why (what makes this timely or urgent?)
- **A** — Avatar callout (who is this for, named directly?)
- **G** — Goal (what specific outcome?)
- **I** — Interval (in how long?)
- **C** — Container word (what is the thing? System, Sprint, Blueprint, Accelerator, Session, etc.)

Use 3–5 components per name. Bonus points for rhyme, alliteration, or a strong number. Examples: "90-Minute AI OS Quick-Start," "The Solo Coach Lead Machine," "7-Day Booking Sprint."

---

## Step 10: Opening Price

Set the suggested opening price. It must satisfy two rules:
1. At minimum 10x perceived ROI vs. the client's dream outcome value (if they save $2k/month, $200/month is priced correctly; if they gain a $10k client, $500–$1,000 is priced correctly)
2. The value stack total must be at minimum 5x the asking price

State the suggested price and briefly explain the ROI anchor.

## Hard Rules

- The offer must have a name. No unnamed offers.
- The value stack total must be at minimum 5x the asking price. If it is not, add more low-cost/high-value components.
- Never price on time. Price on outcome value.
- The guarantee must be conditional — not unconditional (too risky at early stage) and not absent (too weak to convert).
- Every obstacle listed in Step 3 must have a corresponding solution in Step 4. No unaddressed doubts.
- If running standalone: ask for the project folder path if not provided in $ARGUMENTS.
