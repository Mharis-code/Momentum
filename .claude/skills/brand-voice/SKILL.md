# Brand Voice — Build the Personal Brand Foundation

## What This Skill Does

Brand Voice is the foundation layer for the Solopreneur OS. It builds everything that makes content and outreach more effective: the story that earns trust, the positioning that attracts the right people, the content pillars that create consistency, the bios that convert visitors to followers, and the rules that make every post sound like the same person wrote it.

Run this once. Reference the output in every other agent. Update it when the niche or offer changes.

**What you get at the end:**
- A signature story (200–300 words, written for spoken or written use)
- A positioning one-liner ("I help [avatar] achieve [outcome] without [obstacle]")
- 3–5 content pillars with reasoning for each
- Platform bios for LinkedIn, TikTok, Instagram, and Skool — each with an embedded CTA
- A tone/voice guide (5 rules: what you say, what you never say)
- A 7-11-4 gap audit (are you hitting the threshold on each platform?)

## Embedded Knowledge — The 7-11-4 Framework (Daniel Priestley)

To move someone from a stranger to a buyer, you need to accumulate three thresholds with them:

- **7 hours** of shared time (watching, reading, listening to you)
- **11 interactions** (comments, replies, DMs, views, link clicks)
- **4 locations** (distinct platforms or contexts where they've encountered you)

Once a person crosses these thresholds, they stop feeling like they're talking to a vendor. They feel like they know you. Marketing to them stops feeling like marketing — it feels like a friend reaching out.

**The binge-readiness test:** Can someone discover you today and spend an entire day consuming your content? If not, you cannot build the 7-11-4 threshold at scale. This is the reason to be on multiple platforms: each is one location. Cross-posting and repurposing creates the multi-location exposure that accelerates trust.

**Specific targets for a solopreneur:**
- LinkedIn posts + YouTube videos clock toward the 7-hour goal
- Comments, replies, and DMs count as interactions (11-interaction goal)
- LinkedIn, TikTok, Skool, Instagram, and events = location diversity (4-location goal)

The goal is NOT to go viral. The goal is to ensure that 500–2,000 ideal buyers have been 7-11-4-ed before any paid offer is launched. Those are the only people who matter.

## Embedded Knowledge — Signature Story Structure

A signature story has five elements. It is not a resume summary. It is the origin story of a person who understands a specific problem because they lived it.

1. **Origin** — Where you started. What you were doing, what you believed. Make it relatable to the avatar. Do not start with credentials.
2. **Villain / Problem** — What broke, what failed, what you couldn't figure out, what everyone else was doing that wasn't working. The villain can be a system, an assumption, an industry, or a moment of failure.
3. **Transformation** — The thing you discovered, built, or realized. The specific shift that changed everything. Be concrete — not "I learned mindset" but "I built a system that eliminated 3 hours of manual work per day."
4. **Proof** — One specific result. Numbers are better than adjectives. "Saved 10 hours/week" beats "dramatically improved efficiency."
5. **Teaching** — Why you're sharing this. What you now help others do. This is the bridge to the offer.

Rules for the signature story:
- First person throughout
- No corporate jargon
- One idea per sentence
- The villain must be something the avatar also believes is broken
- The proof must be a result the avatar wants

## Embedded Knowledge — Content Pillars

Content pillars are 3–5 topics a creator owns. They sit at the intersection of:
- Your expertise (what you know deeply)
- Your audience's need (what they're trying to solve)
- Your personal interest (what you won't burn out talking about)

A pillar is not a category like "AI" or "business." A pillar is a specific angle or point of view. Examples:
- "Why most AI tools don't stick" (trust/adoption problems)
- "How solopreneurs multiply capacity without hiring" (systems angle)
- "The gap between knowing AI exists and actually using it" (education angle)

Each pillar should be a territory you can own — meaning you'll post about it 50+ times without repeating yourself, and your audience will associate that topic with you.

A content pillar mix for authority: 60% give (education, tools, frameworks), 30% stories (case studies, personal failures, wins), 10% ask (soft CTAs, invitations to engage).

## Step 2: Intake (6 Questions)

Ask one at a time. Wait for each answer before asking the next.

**Question 1:** What do you do, and who do you do it for? (plain language — not a formal bio)

**Question 2:** What did your journey look like before getting here? What did you try that didn't work, or what moment made you change direction? (this becomes the signature story)

**Question 3:** What is the one specific transformation you help people achieve? What do they have after working with you that they didn't have before?

**Question 4:** What are you genuinely fascinated by — topics you'd talk about for free, for hours, to anyone who'd listen?

**Question 5:** Where are you currently showing up online, and what platforms are you planning to be on?

**Question 6:** What do you want people to do when they encounter you for the first time? (follow, book a call, download something, reply to a post)

## Step 3: Confirm Profile

Do not write any brand assets until confirmed.

## Step 4: Build the Signature Story

Using answers from Questions 1–3, write the signature story using the five-element structure: Origin → Villain/Problem → Transformation → Proof → Teaching.

Length: 200–300 words. Written in first person. No em dashes. No corporate jargon.

Write it in two formats:
1. **Written version** — for LinkedIn, bio, or website About page
2. **Spoken summary** — 4–5 sentences that can be delivered in under 30 seconds (for intro videos, DMs, or calls)

---

## Step 5: Build the Positioning One-Liner

Formula: **"I help [specific avatar] [achieve specific outcome] [without specific obstacle or trade-off]."**

The avatar must be specific enough that they recognize themselves. Not "entrepreneurs" — "solopreneurs who want to stop being the bottleneck in their own business."

The outcome must be a destination, not a process. Not "learn AI" — "build an AI system that runs their operations while they focus on client work."

The obstacle must be the one they're most afraid of or resigned to. Not "effort" — "without having to learn to code or hire a team."

Generate 3 options. Ask the person to select or combine.

---

## Step 6: Define Content Pillars

Using answers from Questions 3 and 4, identify 3–5 content pillars.

For each pillar:
- Name it with a specific angle (not a category)
- Write one sentence explaining what territory it covers
- Write one sentence explaining why this person is credible to own it
- Give 2 example post topics from this pillar

Confirm the pillars before proceeding.

## Step 7: Write Platform Bios

Write a bio for each platform the person mentioned in Question 5. Use the confirmed positioning one-liner as the spine. End each bio with the CTA identified in Question 6.

**LinkedIn** (up to 220 characters for the headline; up to 2,200 characters for the About section):
- Headline: positioning one-liner, compressed to fit
- About section: signature story + 3 content pillars + social proof line + CTA

**TikTok** (160 characters maximum — hard limit):
- One-liner of what you do + CTA. No filler.

**Instagram** (150 characters maximum):
- One-liner + CTA. Platform-native tone (more visual, slightly warmer).

**Skool** (up to 250 characters):
- Peer-sharing tone. What you know and what you teach. No self-promotion language.

Flag if any bio exceeds its platform limit and offer a tighter version.

---

## Step 8: Build the Tone/Voice Guide

Write 5 rules for how this person writes. Two categories: what they say, and what they never say.

Structure each rule as:
- **Rule:** the behavior
- **Example:** one sentence showing it in practice

Examples of good rules:
- "Short sentences. One idea per sentence. If a sentence has two ideas, split it."
- "Lead with the problem, not the solution. The reader has to feel the pain before the fix matters."
- "Never use jargon without an immediate plain-English explanation in the next sentence."
- "No em dashes. No 'delve.' No corporate buzzwords."
- "Strong opening line first. Cut the throat-clearing."

Tailor these rules to the actual answers given — the tone guide should feel like it was written for this specific person, not a generic template.

## Hard Rules

- Never write the signature story in third person. Always first person.
- The positioning one-liner must name a specific avatar. "Entrepreneurs" is not specific enough.
- TikTok bio: 160 characters is a hard limit. Flag and trim if exceeded.
- Instagram bio: 150 characters is a hard limit. Flag and trim if exceeded.
- The tone guide rules must be specific and testable — not "be authentic" or "be engaging."
- Do not produce any assets until the intake is confirmed in Step 3.
- If the person cannot identify a specific transformation in Step 2 (Question 3), do not guess. Ask: "What do clients have after working with you that they didn't have before? Give me the most specific version you can."
